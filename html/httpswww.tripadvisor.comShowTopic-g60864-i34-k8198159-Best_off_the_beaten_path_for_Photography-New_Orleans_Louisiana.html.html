      Best off the beaten path for Photography
      4 years ago
      Save

      Hi, all. Headed down there next week. Convention first half, then on my own for Thursday through the weekend. That back half is specific to my interest in photography; more than a hobby for me (my day work that keeps me in lenses LOL).

      I'm landscape and nature shooter, with a side order of urban. I love offbeat, quirky views. There will be the obligatory French Quarter coverage, of course. I want to get to the Audubon, and the sculpture garden. If there are any photographers in the crowd with some ideas, I'd be grateful for the sharing. I'm respectful of my environment, so if there's something that demands we not broadcast the GPS, I would welcome a PM. And it's not all about pretty pictures; reality is often a part of my portfolio.

      I expect to rent a car, I am still researching plantation locales for local interest (Oak Alley looks stunning). Ideally, I would like to try an early morning bayou shot, or at least a back lit shot, so if you have some directions to send me to a landing spot, I'd be most grateful. We Canadians don't get a lot of that kind of foliage up here LOL. Gonna cram in information in the new few days, but I know how good TA advisors are are about local recommendations.

      Grateful for any help you can offer. It's been roughly 40 years since the last time I was there, I expect things have changed a bit. Looking forward to it, immensely.

      As a retired advertising photographer, I have always loved shooting the street scenes, prior to the city coming to life - the deliveries in the FQ, the trucks in the French Market, the folk washing down the sidewalks in front of ____ .

      I also have greatly enjoyed photographing the signage around New Orleans - the old painted signs on various buildings, as well as the neon signs all around.

      Then, dawn around the bridges, from downtown New Orleans, out to the Huey P Long, in Jefferson Parish, have always been a draw for me.

      The Algiers Ferry, early in the AM is also quite interesting, as are the streetcars on most routes.

      Enjoy,

      Hunt

      Also, on the West side, the towns of Breaux Bridge and New Iberia are right at the edge of the Basin, and well worth exploring too.

      In general, the Basin is crossed by US Hwy 190 to the North, by I-10 in sort of the upper-middle, and then by US Hwy 90 at the Southern end. Several picturesque little state hwys, like LA 70 on the East side, and LA 345 on the West, skirt the area, and pass though some quaint little villages. Photographic opportunities exist all around the area.

      Travel safely,

      Hunt
