      sometimes you just really want a plain donut. Nothing over-the-top and fancy like the $6 ones you find everywhere else. I found this place because of yelp. My son and I enjoyed picking out some fresh and tasty donuts...and maybe even picked out a couple more to take home to the rest of the family.

      These donuts are both great tasting and fairly priced, a combination I rarely find these days. How a company believes that $3-$4 for a single donut is a reasonable price is well beyond me. I was about to stop with five donuts and the counter person told me that there was a price break if I added one more, and with that it was less than $1.50 for each regular donut and an extra $0.50 for their specialty pastries. I did spend the extra to try their buttermilk cakes. While they were pretty good, I won't get more of them the next time. They have a good concept, a bit of fruit filling in a fairly massive buttermilk cake. Not my preferred ratio of dough to filling (too much dough) to justify the extra price. The service was friendly, quick and the selection was pretty good even coming in after dinner when most donut places have been pretty picked over in terms of selection. The wife's favorite is a chocolate old fashioned and they met her expectations with a great one. The maple buttermilk bar was probably my favorite and both my kids were happy with their choices. Certainly with another try

      The best doughnuts and cheeseburger on the plant. We are so lucky to have these talented bakers in Portland. The owners are Mexican with a European history. I am so impressed by the quality of the food. A simple cheeseburger is a test. This one had perfect bun, yeasty and soft and a medium beef patty (slightly pink) with perfect melted cheese. The doughnuts have an amazing crusty exterior ( the buttermilk group I think) and the jelly doughnut had a perfect cherry filling...so fresh and the perfect sweetness and texture. The meat on the cheese burger was better than many Portland Restaurants. Great service in a gem of a deceptively simple and highly refined small old fashioned doughnut/ice cream HEAVEN

      I love this city's upscale ice cream, but sometimes to you don't need $3-$5 cones, especially for a child. I like this city's fancy super-sweet doughnuts, but I won't pay $3 for a doughnut. And the bigger issue, more than cost, with most ice cream parlors and doughnut shops, is that you really aren't welcome to linger: the locale is small, the seats aren't comfortable, and its chilly inside. You're conscious that their business relies on turning over your table rapidly. Not Angel's. There's plenty of tables. There's Umpqua ice cream and homemade doughnuts. There's coffee and warm cheap meals. There's a bathroom that works. There is a feeling that it is a refuge -- you can stay for awhile, if you need to. Or you can do a speedy transaction to satisfy your sweet tooth at an Old Portland price. I wish more neighborhoods in Portland were looked over by an Angel's.

      Specialties

      Life is sweet at Angel's Donuts & Ice Cream! We've been making our neighbors delicious treats since 2004 and loving every minute of it. From our famous fresh-baked donuts to our indulgent sundaes to our sandwiches, we're serving it up all day. As a family-owned business, our goal is to make you feel like family when you come in, too. We may have changed our look and our name, but we'll always have the same folks behind the counter, the same recipes for every treat, and the same welcoming attitudes that we've been known for for more than 10 years.

      History

      Established in 2004.

      I would like to begin by saying Angels Donuts and Ice Cream (formally Tonallis Donuts and Cream) in Portland is and has never been associated with either Tonallis Donuts in Vancouver, Washington.

      We First began our business as Tonallis Donuts and Cream in 2004; owned by two brothers and a two friends. The store was named after one of the friends. For the past 8 years Tonallis was solely owned by one of the brothers after the other 3 lost vision of what Tonallis could become. After 12 years of being named Tonallis after one of the former partners, we have decided to rebrand, remodel and Rename the business after our son, who is now operating the business in Portland. We are now branching off into Vancouver and hope we can continue to see the same success as we do in Portland. Thank you all for business and support and we hope we can continue to give you the best quality donuts, ice cream and service we possibly can.
